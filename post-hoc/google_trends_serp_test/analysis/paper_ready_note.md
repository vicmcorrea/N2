# Short Paper-ready Note

As a conservative post hoc probe, we compared the frozen 55-lemma comparison panel against Google Trends series retrieved through Oxylabs for Brazil over 2004–2023. This signal should not be treated as external ground truth for semantic change. Google Trends reflects normalized public search interest rather than lexical usage, and Oxylabs explicitly warns that its `google_trends_explore` output may differ from direct browser use of Google Trends. We therefore interpret it only as an external attention signal.

Under that interpretation, the relationship to the frozen internal rankings is mixed. `Word2Vec` drift terms show somewhat greater external temporal movement than stable controls, but the association is only moderate at the full-panel level and weak within the drift set itself. `TF-IDF` candidates are more heterogeneous: some publicly salient issue terms such as `previdência`, `preço`, and `saúde` show clear external movement, while others such as `trabalhador` and `eleição` remain comparatively flat. Stable controls are not uniformly flat in Google Trends, and theory seeds are also mixed. Overall, the Google signal offers selective contextual resonance for some politically salient terms, but it does not provide a strong external validation benchmark for the frozen semantic-drift findings.
